GCS-Tacotron: End-to-End Code-Switched TTS with Graph Representation of Prosody Boundary

Abstract

    Code-switched text-to-speech (TTS) has attract more and more attentions in recent years since the increasing international exchanges. One of the challenges of code-switched TTS is the naturalness and fluency when switching between different languages.In order to synthesize smooth code-switched speech, we propose a novel code-switched TTSmodel, called GCS-Tacotron. It is a variant of code-switched Tacotron for Mandarin-English mixed-lingual TTS that introduces prosodic information via graph neural network (GNN). In GCS-Tacotron, we first extend the prosodic graph for Mandarintext to Mandarin-English mixed-lingual text, then encode the prosodic graph with GNN, and integrate graphical representations of code-switched prosodic information to the model in two ways: one is to add a Graph Auxiliary Encoder (GAE), and the other is the simplified Serial Graph Encoder (GAE). The pro-posed models are trained with available monolingual corpora. Experiments show that the proposed models can not only synthesize natural code-switched speech, but also can synthesize speech with a mixture of Mandarin and English-alphabet.

Intex Term —— text-to-speech, code-switched, graph neural network
