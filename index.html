End-to-End Code-Switched TTS with Graph Representation of Prosody Boundary

Abstract

    Neural Text-to-Speech (TTS) technology has made considerable progress, capable of synthesizing natural speech that is close to human voice. Moreover, the application of Graph Neural Network on TTS opens up an alternative way of prosody modeling. However, the prominent TTS models only have a good performance on monolingual. As international exchanges become closer and closer, there are many scenarios that require cross-lingual TTS. When applied them to code-switched speech synthesis, they often synthesize unacceptable results. In this paper, we proposed a code-switched TTS model, GCS-Tacotron, which can synthesize Mandarin-English code-switched speech by adding explicit phoneme-level language embedding, and uses GNN to model semantic and prosodic information in the spatial domain according to the hierarchical prosodic boundary of the text. And, without affecting the sound quality, the structure of the Graph Encoder in GCS-Tacotron is further simplified. The model is trained on available monolingual corpus. The experiments show that the proposed model can not only synthesize natural code-switched speech, but also can synthesize speech with a mixture of Mandarin and English-letter.
